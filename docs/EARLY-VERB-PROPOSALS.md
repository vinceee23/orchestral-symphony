# Early Active-Verb — proposals for Vince to pick (then build)

> ## 📜 HISTORICAL 2026-06-29 — outcome recorded
> Option A (**Warm-Up**) was chosen and built — then **RETIRED 2026-06-29** as redundant with
> Conduct/Crescendo (commit `bba0415`). The L0–L3 active verb is now **Conduct/Crescendo** (post-MO).
> This doc is kept for the decision record only; see `HANDOFF.md` for current state.

**Why this exists:** the appeal review flagged that L0–L3's moment-to-moment is essentially "buy tier → wait
→ prestige," with **Conduct (hold-to-conduct) as the *only* active verb — and idle sustains ~70% of it**, so
even that flattens. For a min-maxing audience the early loop reads as "undercooked." We want **one
distinctive, recurring active *decision*** in the early game — idle-FIRST (optional amplifier, never
demanded), and ideally one that **foreshadows a later layer** (mechanic = story beat). Per the no-build-
without-sign-off rule, these are PROPOSALS — **Vince picks one (or a blend), then it's built + sim-gated.**

Constraints any pick must honor: idle-first (works AFK at a baseline; active amplifies, never gates),
not twitchy (strategic, not reflex), small build, fits the orchestral theme, doesn't pile onto the single
production funnel (use a bounded/separate effect).

---

## Option A — "Warm-Up" (foreshadows L5 Virtuoso's Perfect Take) — RECOMMENDED
A small **consistency meter**: when you hold your production *steady/in-tempo* for a short window (no big
spikes, not idle-dark), a **Warm-Up** bar fills and grants a modest temporary production bonus that decays
when you stop engaging. It's a tiny, early taste of L5's "score the evenness of your playing."
- **Active decision:** do I ride a steady line for the warm-up bonus, or spike-buy now?
- **Idle-fit:** strong — idle sits at a baseline warm-up floor (small bonus); active holding earns the peak.
- **Foreshadow:** literally the seed of L5 Perfect Take — when L5 lands, the player already "gets" it.
- **Build:** small (one scalar meter + a steadiness check + a capped temp mult). Lowest-risk of the three.
- **Risk:** must not become mandatory babysitting — cap the active:idle gap tight (~1.3–1.5×).

## Option B — "Phrasing" (active Conduct upgrade)
Turn Conduct from a passive hold into a light **on-beat phrasing** input: tapping in time with the tempo
(generous windows, not a rhythm game) boosts the crescendo more than holding. A natural extension of the
verb that already exists.
- **Active decision:** engage with the beat for extra crescendo vs let it idle-conduct.
- **Idle-fit:** good — auto-conduct keeps the baseline; phrasing is the amplifier.
- **Foreshadow:** ties to Percussion/Timpana (the Pulse) — a light seed of the rhythm-god domain.
- **Build:** small-medium (a beat clock + input-timing → crescendo bonus). Accessibility: needs a
  hold-free/auto fallback so it's never *required* (timing input can exclude some players).
- **Risk:** edges toward twitchy; must stay forgiving + optional.

## Option C — "Cues" (narrative micro-decisions)
Periodically the gatekeeper/the music surfaces a short **cue** — a 1-tap choice that bends the next stretch
(e.g. "the crowd leans toward the low strings — indulge them?" → a temporary trade-off bonus). A light
*strategic* beat woven into the narrative.
- **Active decision:** a real (small) choice every few minutes, tied to the story voice.
- **Idle-fit:** strong — ignore a cue and it lapses to a neutral default; no penalty for idling.
- **Foreshadow:** reinforces the narrator-as-presence (and could seed L4 domain/identity leanings).
- **Build:** medium (a cue scheduler + a small authored cue pool + the choice→effect plumbing + UI).
  Most content-authoring of the three; risks feeling like a side-quest if overdone.
- **Risk:** can read as interruptions if too frequent; keep sparse + skippable.

---

## My recommendation
**Option A (Warm-Up).** It's the smallest build, the most idle-safe, and it earns double duty — a genuine
early active decision AND a foreshadow of L5, which directly serves the locked north-star ("the mechanic IS
the story beat"). B is a nice *complement* later (it makes Conduct less flat); C is the richest but the most
authoring + interruption-risk. **Pick one to build now; A + a later B is a strong combo.**
